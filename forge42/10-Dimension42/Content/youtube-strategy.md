# YouTube Strategy: Dimension42

Faceless tech channel under the D42 brand. A content factory — not a personal brand channel.

## Channel Identity

- **Handle:** @dimension42ai
- **Format:** 100% AI-produced (with editorial direction)
- **Face:** Never. No personal brand. This is a media property.
- **Voice:** One consistent AI voice — pick it and make it the channel identity. Clone Marty's voice if desired (YouTube allows without disclosure as of 2026).

## Content Pillars

- Self-hosting AI stacks (Ollama, LiteLLM, Docker)
- Cheap model routing and inference optimization
- Building software with AI agents (OpenClaw, Eddie)
- Zero-human infrastructure blueprints
- Tool and platform reviews (what actually works for a one-person AI company)

## Posting Cadence

- Long-form: 2–3 per week
- Shorts: Daily (repurposed clips from long-form)

## Cross-Posting

- Shorts → TikTok, Instagram Reels, X
- D42 channel never references Marty by name — standalone brand
- Marty channel naturally references D42: "I built this using my Dimension42 platform..."

## Content Safety Guidelines

1. **Disclose AI-generated content** using YouTube's "altered content" setting when content could be mistaken for real events or real people
2. **Never batch-upload** more than 2–3 videos per day (algorithm flags content factories)
3. **Original editorial direction** on every video — unique research, data, or analysis. No prompt-and-publish.
4. **Custom thumbnails** on every video — no templated look-alikes
5. **Script with intention** — pacing, emphasis, rhythm. Treat AI voice like a voice actor you're directing.

## Launch Sequence

1. Produce 5–8 videos before launch (content buffer)
2. Establish consistent AI voice and thumbnail style
3. Launch with 3 videos in the first week
4. Maintain 2–3/week cadence before considering Shorts program
