# Zero Human Narrative

The central brand story for Marty Sampson. This is the most compelling differentiator and should be the narrative spine of everything on the personal brand.

## The Story

> "I'm building a software company, a media company, and a consulting practice with zero employees. Every role is filled by an AI agent. I'm the only human. Here's what I'm learning."

This isn't a gimmick — it's the actual operating model. The story gets more valuable as the build progresses. Every milestone, every failure, every tool deployed is a content piece.

## Where It Lives

| Channel | How it shows up |
|---|---|
| martysampson.com | Featured prominently on the homepage |
| YouTube (Marty channel) | Primary narrative arc — the story unfolds in real time |
| Substack | Long-form essays about decisions, failures, and insights |
| X | Short updates, milestones, behind-the-scenes screenshots |
| LinkedIn | Professional credibility and consulting lead generation |

## Where It Does NOT Live

- **Dimension42** — D42 is a tech brand. It delivers value. It doesn't need to explain who runs it.
- **37Metrics** — The holding company's staffing model is nobody's business.

## Content Angles

Use these to generate episodes, essays, and posts:

- "Month [N]: I built an AI agent that [does X]. Here's what happened."
- "My AI [CFO/PM/support agent] just [did X]. What I learned."
- "Zero employees, $[X] revenue: What I've learned running a company with AI agents."
- "I gave my AI [role] a mandate. This is what it did."
- "The hardest part of building with AI agents isn't the tech — it's [human thing]."
- "Why I'm building in public: the zero-human company as a documentary."

## The Director Role: What Marty Actually Does

The zero-human narrative is more than a brand story — it's a precise operational model. Understanding what the one human actually does is what makes the model credible.

**Marty is a director, not an executor.** He seeds intent and approves at defined checkpoints. Everything between those checkpoints runs autonomously.

### The 7 Standard Checkpoints

| # | Checkpoint | Before what |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Approve Vision Brief + Business Brief | Product definition begins |
| 2 | Approve PRD | Design and architecture begin |
| 3 | Approve System Architecture + ADRs | Implementation begins |
| 4 | Approve full handoff package | Dev Suite (Claude Code) starts |
| 5 | Evaluate each shippable increment | Each release is cut |
| 6 | Approve security/legal documents | Publication |
| 7 | Review post-mortems + retrospectives | After incidents; quarterly |

**Between checkpoints:** Everything runs autonomously. Agents surface questions rather than guessing. If a decision isn't in the handoff package, it routes to Marty — it doesn't get resolved by assumption.

**Hard lines — always human, no exceptions:**
- Legal document review (DPA, ToS, Privacy Policy)
- Security document sign-off (threat model, access control)
- Post-mortem narrative

This is the operational proof of concept for the zero-human story. Not "AI does things while I watch" — "I make decisions at the right level and AI executes everything in between."

## Guardrails

- **Ship first, talk second.** The narrative only works if you're actually building things. Don't let content outpace execution.
- **Product bugs stay separate.** D42 products just need to work. Don't advertise "zero support team" on the product itself — save that for the Marty brand story.
- **The connection is legitimate.** YouTube and other platforms may surface that Marty Sampson runs Dimension42. That's fine — it's true, and transparency reinforces the brand.
